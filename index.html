About Me

A UX Designer based in Des Moines, Iowa

In high school, I learned I had a knack for public speaking, storytelling, and audience analysis. When I would present Rube Goldberg machines at the University of Illinois Engineering Week, each time I spoke I would mix up my speech depending on who was passing by the project. You wouldn't explain a machine the same way to a 5 year old with their grandparents and a tenured engineering professor.

I've grown my speaking and audience analysis skills since then, and learned a lot more about design, research, and programming. I believe these combination of skills make me a great UX designer. I'm able to make great designs using data to inform my decisions and create my deliverables so both business stakeholders and developers understand and agree with the end result.

During the day, I am a UX designer. But by night (and sometimes by day), I'm also a:

I'm passionate about good product design, digital or physical. I enjoy noticing everything from the tiny, add-on details to the overall flow of product. I'm constantly looking for well-designed technology, not only thinking about why it's well designed, but also how I would improve it.
