About Me

Growing Up

I grew up in a small town just outside of Champaign, Illinois.

I played travel softball for 10 years. Becuase of this I learned how to be competitive, work well on a team, and manage my time.

For a few years I was also the speaker for an engineering club that presented a Rube Goldberg machine at the University of Illinois Engineering Week. I gave speeches explaing our machine to people ranging from the engineering professors to five year olds exploring with their grandparents. This taught me how to speak well in public and cater information to my audience.


In College

I started off as an English Education major because I like writing and teaching, but quickly switched to technical communication because it combined writing and design. I love the creative and analytical aspects of my major. My core classes are in rhetoric, but I also get to learn about programming, digital media, writing and editing, and human-computer interaction.

I'm kept pretty busy between doing homework, being the president for the Society for Technical Communication and the outreach chair for the web development club, but in my free time I edit videos for the English department and I am learning to prefect my waffle making skills.


Currently

I am in my last semester of college. I will graduate May 7. While I am currently working for the English department as a video editor and the mechanical engineering department as an instructional designer, they are part time jobs for students. After I graduate I would like to work as a user experience designer or a multimedia specialist. If those don't work out, the dream job I've had since I was 2 is to be a pirate.
