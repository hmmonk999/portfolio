Redesign

Solutions to Found Problems

  • Replace the only image on the page.
    Reason: The image violates sections 1.1.1 and 1.4.5 of the WCAG 2.0 standards and 100% of tested users disliked the look of the image.
  • Darken the links in the breadcrumb menu.
    Reason: The low contrast between the background color and the font color violates section 1.4.3 of the WCAG 2.0 standards.
  • Restructure the look and code of the headers in the design.
    Reason: In the code the h2 (heading level two) is skipped and may confuse users with screen readers.
    Reason: The design of the headers is also not consistant in design. One link is structed like a header and confused 100% of tested users.
  • Reformat the paragraphs so users can scan the page instead of needing to read the page to understand the content.
    Reason: 2/3 of tested users did said they did not like the way the information was structured.
    Reason: Research has shown 79% of people scan websites by looking at lists, bolding keywords, and employing meaningful sub headings.
  • Add images of current students.
    Reason: The tone both stakeholders wanted to convey was friendly and welcoming. Images of smiling students will help convey this.
    Reason: In the user test, two users said they would like to see images of students.
  • Add section for current courses and course plans
    Reason: Adds course requirements so the page title is accurate.
    Reason: Reformats the links which confused users in the original design.
