Wellmark Blue Cross Blue Shield: Project Approval Methodology


Back to UX Projects
Industry Healthcare Insurance, Internal Processes
My Deliverables Project Approval Workshop, Project Submission Form Analysis
Team Me (UX Designer), Dennis (UX Team Lead)
Duration 3 Weeks (August 2021)

Project Background

Wellmark Blue Cross Blue Shield is a health insurance non-profit in the United States with more than two million members and over 2,000 employees. Internally, any Wellmark employee or contractor can submit ideas for improvements or additions to the different applications Wellmark has. A group of UX and business leaders come together to discuss which ideas should be explored further. Anyone who submits an idea has to fill out:

  • a description of the idea
  • the audience
  • how it improves the user's experience
  • how it helps the business

Our Problem Statement

UX and business leaders in the pre-project approval meetings are struggling to decided on which ideas will provide the most value to the users and the business.

Audience

  1. Dennis, UX Team Lead (Facilitator)
  2. Project managers and other project specific employees

Workshop Goals

  1. Facilate discussion about why an idea for a project is something a team should devote time to scoping and creating a more detailed solution
  2. More quickly decide if an idea is worth exporting

My Challenges

  1. The soludtion had to be problem egnostic.
  2. The solution had to be usable by remote users
  3. Prior to this project, I had little knowledge about project decision making tools.

The Solution

A Mural workshop for the UX team and stakeholders to discuss project ideas at a high level

Why A Workshop?

This project initially started out as a document creation project. I was assigned to create these documents because of my writing and document design skills. But the meeting to discuss project ideas requires lots of input and consensus from many departments at Wellmark. It needed to be something that was interactive and helped faciliate discussion This workshops helps provide the structure needed for in-depth group discission to make sure Wellmark is solving the right problems with the most value.

Part 1: Problem Statement

Define the Audience and Problem

Because the problem and audience are already defined in the submission form, this part of the workshop is to make sure everyone is one the same page about what project idea and the high level details by writing out Wellmark's prefered syntax for the problem statement.

"As a [audience type] I experience [pain point(s)] when I [current state of interaction(s)] with Wellmark."


Part 2: Root Cause Analysis

Decide What The Actual Problem Is

The "but why" technique helps the UX team and our stakeholders dig deeper to find the cause of the problem to make sure we are solving for the right think and have a thorough understanding of the issues our users are having.


Everyone writes down what they think is causing the problem statement

Everyone votes on the cause that would solve the most problems if fixed


Part 3: Force Field Analysis

Decide If The Problem Will Create Enough Value

Once the team has decided what the problem is, they can start looking into the value that would be provided by solving it. A force field analysis helps weight the pros and cons by add a point value to each "driving force" and "resisting force" so there can be more emphasis placed on the importance of

Everyone writes down all of the driving or restraining forces of a project then they group the similar forces
The group comes together to score the driving and restraining forces of the project
