Duration 5 Months (March - August 2021), 4 hours a week

Company Background

Lapis is an early stage health tech start-up trying to helping minority Americans beat heart disease. Around 647,0000 people die from heart disease every year. Over 50% of the deaths are people of color, yet the majority of digital solutions are not designed to fit the needs of diverse and underserved populations. It started off as a habit tracking app with a focus around health. In May of 2021, Michelle, a reventive Cardiology Dietician with over 10 years of experience working with heart disease patients, joined the team and the app went under a complete structural and design upgrade.

Now the app is focused on lowering blood pressure. The app suggests habits in 3 out of 6 possible lifestyle areas (exercise, diet, hydration, sleep, stress, or smoking) that the user needs the most improvement in and they track their habits for 14 day periods, along with their blood pressure, to help improve their heart condition. The team is prepping for a beta launch in October and a pilot study in Janurary

User Interviews from Lapis Alpha Testing

Prior to being hired, the Lapis app was in alpha testings. Peter interviewed the 24 people testing the app about the biggest challeges they faced when trying to change their lifestyle to help with their heart health. I helped analyze it so we could use it to make more informed decisions for the app.

Over 90% (22/24) felt they didn't understand or know how to use the information they were given by their doctor.

Empathy Maps from Qualitative Academic Studies

Because the Lapis app has only been in alpha testing, I wanted to make sure we had as much information as possible about potential users so we could better empathise with them. There have been many qualitiative studies about how low and moderate income black Americans think and feel about their lifestyle and its relationship to risks of heart disease.

Define What Our Target Audience Needs

Because of how many users felt frustred and confused about where to start, we knew two things:

  1. Educational content needed to be a huge part of the app.
  2. We needed to make suggests on where and how to start making small life style changes.
