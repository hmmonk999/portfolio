Web Design Intern

Iowa Department of Transportation | May 2019 - September 2019

Over the course of the 2019 Summer, I worked as a web design intern for the Iowa Department of Transportation. My main project was to work on a website redesign for the Research Department and in my down time, I worked on smaller projects for the web design team in the Strategic Communication Department.


Major Projects

DOT Research Department Webpages Redesign

The Research Department wanted to redesign their webpages. I created a design in Adobe XD with inspiration from Google Analytics, other state DOT websites, and current web design trends. After the design was finalized, I made updates to temporary CMS pages to practice then the real CMS using HMTL, CSS, JavaScript, and DotNetNuke tools.


DOT Homepage Reorganization Suggestions

The third click into the DOT website is a link farm. I created a site map, used Google Analytics, and analyzed user survey answers to make suggestions on how to shorten the list of links and the homepage design.

Iowa DOT Homepage Redesign Suggestions

https://iowadot.gov/
Purpose

A user survey done in December of 2018 showed that many users were upset or confused by the organization of the DOT website. While the homepage is fairly organized, most of the pages after this are just filled with too many links. My goal for this project was to make several suggestions on how to organize the website better.

Audience

Anyone in the state of Iowa may visit the DOT website. Most of these people will be drivers. The age range can be 14 or 15 year olds looking for information about driver's permits to elderly people looking at how often they need to renew their driver's license. One interesting, common experience shared by all audience that I needed to consider was the Iowa weather.

Process

To make suggestions, I needed to understand the entire website better. I created a site map in excel to see where all the links went, count the number of repeated links, and understand how the website is currently organized. After I created the site map, I created a new organization scheme with no repeated links. I then looked at results from a user survey to see where frustation points were and what they suggested could be better about the homepage design and organization. I presented my findings on my last day.

Suggestions
  1. Create a large search bar in the center of the homepage
  2. Have a link on the homepage that links to the road conditions, especially during the winter.
  3. Lower the opacity to make words more readable
  4. Use a mega menu or bread crumbs to organize the website instead of a large list of links
